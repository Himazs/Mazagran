About Us

Our Story

Dunedin's Living Coffee Institution

At Mazagran Cafe Espresso Bar, coffee isn't treated as a modern, automated transaction — it's handled as an art form. Nestled tightly into the cultural fabric of Dunedin at 36 Moray Place, our warm, sensory-filled space welcomes anyone searching for an authentic coffee experience. We are fiercely independent and deeply invested in the tactile, old-school rituals of preparation.

Step inside and you will immediately meet the comforting aroma of beans roasting over open flame. We deliberately cultivate an intimate environment where music spins, conversation flows effortlessly, and patrons can watch the entire process unfold from raw green bean to beautifully extracted espresso shot.

Our Craft

The Craft Behind the Cup

Honoring Traditional Machinery

We reject the modern trend of computerized, push-button coffee making. Instead, Mazagran relies purely on sensory feedback and beautifully preserved, traditional mechanical hardware to coax out the finest properties of every single bean.

Vintage In-House Roaster

Where the magic begins. We roast manually by sight, smell, and sound right in front of your eyes.

Mechanical Lever Extraction

Our classic manual machinery grants total tactile control over pressure profiles for a perfect crema.

The Glass Cabinet

Freshly baked traditional slices, cakes, and tarts prepared fresh daily to accompany your brew.

By adjusting variables with physical gears, manual handles, and keen human intuition, our baristas craft an experience that digital automation simply cannot copy. It is demanding, precise, and wonderfully old-fashioned work — and we wouldn't have it any other way.
